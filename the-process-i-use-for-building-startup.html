The Process I Use For Building My Startup

2021-03-07

The reason I am writing this blog post is because I am a 28 years old person, who have already wasted many years of this life trying to build products that no one needs. And that happened all because I didn't know or even didn't want to know a must read theory of building successful startup.

Maybe this sounds too easy, probably I knew some parts of this theory but I couldn't connect the dots for two reasons: 1. No one explained me clearly why this theory is so important. 2. I was too ignorant person.

Anyway with some experience and hard punches in the past, I decided to write the formula of this theory in this blog post for myself and share it with you. I will call this theory like this: what is the formula of effective and fast way to build a very successful startup.

So let's dive in.

STEP 1. Find what's missing in this world and for what type of customers. (Spend a lot of time on it, especially in the beginning)

STEP 2. Decide what type of product you want to build and for whom.

- Check if a market you've chosen for your idea is good. (See if you can become friends and spend a lot of time talking with someone from your market and/or sell your product in advance.)
IF NOT, GO TO STEP 2. You either are not the right person for this market or the market simply does not want to be innovated.

- Check if you can build this product in weeks or even days.
IF NOT, think honestly if you want to take a risk.

- Think about are there any ways you can grow this startup organically or profitably.
IF NOT, GO TO STEP 2.

- Think realistically how this startup can become a billion dollar company. (Adjacent markets, proprietary technology, branding, network effects, defensibility and etc.)

IF NOT, think if you really want to waste your time on something not very important.

STEP 3. Build MVP using strict timeboxes. Reduce the amount of features and other type of work instead of giving yourself more time to finish.

STEP 4. Find several potential users manually and show them MVP in order to get feedback.

STEP 5. Try to make them buy and/or use your MVP with high engagement for a long time.

DOES IT WORK? IF NOT, GO TO STEP 2.

STEP 6. Try to pick 10-100 users manually and make them buy and/or use your product with high engagement for a long time..

DOES IT WORK? IF NOT, GO TO STEP 2.

STEP 7. Try to find a way to grow organically and/or profitably

DOES IT WORK? IF NOT, GO TO STEP 2.

IF EVERYTHING WORKS, YOU ARE VERY LUCKY!!!

STEP 8. On this step you have a product market fit. Now optimize a product for growth and build a team.

This is the formula I use and will keep using in the future for building my own startups. The faster I will go from one step to another, the more chance I have to become successful.

I hope you've enjoyed it. If you have any questions, feel free to contact me.

P.S. For me personally, the STEP 1. and STEP 2. were the hardest, because I could find plenty of useful information about other steps, but not these two. And I really don't why is that so. Probably many of us don't know.

I strongly believe that the process of finding great ideas and deciding what things to build and for whom is a topic that is extremely underexplored.

I hope I will be able to give you more details about this topic in the future.
